Sample Conversation Scripts — Polyglot Voice Companion

Use these scripts to manually test your agent. Each scenario contains at least one
language switch mid-conversation. Your agent must:
1.	Detect the language change within 1–2 utterances.
2.	Continue the conversation context (do NOT reset memory on switch).
3.	Respond in the language the user just spoke.

Languages used in these samples: English, Hindi, Spanish. You may adapt the
samples to any 3 languages your chosen models support.

— — —

Scenario 1 — Customer Support: Order Status

Turn 1 (English): "Hi, I need to check the status of my order. The order ID is 4421."

*(Agent acknowledges in English, asks for verification.)*

Turn 2 (English): "Yes, the email on the account is rahul@example.com."

*(Agent confirms order is out for delivery, in English.)*

Turn 3 (Hindi): "Theek hai, lekin delivery kal tak ho jaayegi kya?"
*(Translation: "OK, but will the delivery happen by tomorrow?")*

*(Agent must now switch to Hindi, retain order context, answer the delivery question in Hindi.)*

Turn 4 (Hindi): "Aur agar nahi hua toh refund mil sakta hai?"
*(Translation: "And if not, can I get a refund?")*

*(Agent continues in Hindi.)*

Turn 5 (English): "Actually let's switch back — can you email me the tracking link?"

*(Agent must switch back to English, retain context.)*

— — —

Scenario 2 — Travel Planning: Hotel Booking

Turn 1 (Spanish): "Hola, quiero reservar un hotel en Bangalore para el próximo fin de semana."
*(Translation: "Hello, I want to book a hotel in Bangalore for next weekend.")*

*(Agent responds in Spanish.)*

Turn 2 (Spanish): "Para dos personas, presupuesto de 5000 rupias por noche."

*(Agent suggests options in Spanish.)*

Turn 3 (English): "Sorry, my Spanish is rusty. Can we continue in English? Tell me again about the second option."

*(Agent switches to English, recalls "second option" from prior context.)*

Turn 4 (English): "Book it. Confirm the dates please."

— — —

Scenario 3 — Code-Switching Within an Utterance (Stretch)

Turn 1 (Mixed Hindi+English): "Mujhe ek pizza order karna hai, but make it veg only please."

*(Agent should handle mixed-language gracefully — either pick the dominant language
or respond in mixed style. Document your choice.)*

Turn 2 (English): "And add a coke too."

— — —

Scenario 4 — Rapid Switching (Stress Test)

Turn 1 (English): "What's the weather in Mumbai today?"

Turn 2 (Hindi): "Aur Delhi mein?"
*(Translation: "And in Delhi?")*

Turn 3 (Spanish): "¿Y en Chennai?"
*(Translation: "And in Chennai?")*

Turn 4 (English): "Compare all three for me."

*(Agent must answer the comparison in English while having understood three different
languages in the prior turns.)*

Evaluation Checklist for the Panel

For each scenario, the evaluator should observe:

•	[ ] Did the agent detect the language switch correctly?
•	[ ] Was the context (order ID, hotel preference, prior question) carried across the switch?
•	[ ] What was the latency from end-of-user-speech to start-of-agent-speech? (Target: <1.2s, stretch: <800ms)
•	[ ] Did the TTS voice match the language naturally (e.g., Hindi voice for Hindi reply)?
•	[ ] How did the agent handle the code-switching utterance?
•	[ ] Did the agent ever "forget" prior context after a language switch?
